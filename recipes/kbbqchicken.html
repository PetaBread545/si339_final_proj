Easy Korean BBQ Chicken

Description

This sweet and salty barbecue chicken was my first chicken dish I made. It literally takes 3 ingredients and some time to make a delicious entree that goes great with rice or noodles.

Ingredients

  • Korean Barbecue Sauce
  • 2 pounds boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 1 teaspoon cooking oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  • Dice your chicken thighs into bite sized cubes, trimming away as much fat as possible.
  • Add to a bowl/container and drizzle the Korean Barbecue Sauce until all the meat is covered (not submerged!).
  • Use your hands or tongs to mix the chicken, making sure that all surfaces are covered in the sauce
  • Place in the fridge for at least 30 minutes to let marinate.
  • When ready to cook, heat oil on medium-low heat.
  • When hot, add your marinated chicken. Be sure not to add too much (don't let the chicken overlap).
  • Add salt to taste.

Tips and Notes

  • You can use any part of the chicken for this, I've just found that boneless and skinless chicken thighs are the easiest to prep and taste the best.
  • You can add garlic both to the pan when your cooking it or to the chicken while it marinated to enhance flavor.
  • Trimming away the fat is preference. I don't like excess fat on my chicken but it does add flavor and tenderness to the dish as a whole.
  • You can let it sit in the marinate for a day or two but don't take to long to cook it after that as it will go bad.
