Pico de Gallo

Description

Pico de gallo is one of my favorite sides/toppings to make. Its fresh, versatile, simple, and delicious. I like to eat mine with tortilla chips or use it as a topping when I make burrito bowls, tacos, nachos, etc. It only requires 6 ingredients and is super cheap to make.

Ingredients

  • 3 Serrano (spicy) or 3 Jalapeno Peppers (less spicy)
  • 1 lime
  • 4 large roma tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons of salt
  • Bushel of cilantro
  • 1/2 large red onion

Instructions

  1. Start by dicing your tomatoes into little cubes and gather them into a bowl. Size is preference but I like them about 3 millimeters in size.
  2. Next, dice the 3 peppers of choice into cubes of the same size, adding it to the same bowl. It might be a little tricky since their shape is not the same, but just get them to be around the same size.
  3. The onion is up next, dicing to the same size as the other two vegetables and adding them to the bowl.
  4. Chop up some cilantro to add to the bowl, I like to use the leaves of 3-4 branches as the stems can be bitter.
  5. Squeeze one lime's worth of juice over it all.
  6. Add your 2 tablespoons of salt and mix thoroughly.
  7. Let settle in the fridge for at least 20 minutes.

Tips and Notes

  • Some people prefer only the flesh of their tomatoes, which you can do by carving out the seeds and innards of your tomatoes before you dice them.
  • White onions are also an option if you're not a big fan of them but I feel like the flavor and smell of red onion is perfect for pico.
  • Although it won't technically be pico anymore, you can add some canned corn at the end to give little pops of sweetness in your pico.
  • When you eat it, try to get the pieces that have been soaking the in water at the bottom, as those would have absorbed the most lime and salt.
